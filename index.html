About Us

Welcome to The Gluten Free Garden! We are a family with families in mind. Our goal is to educate and demonstrate how to have restaurant quality food at home. Our extensive garden is filed with fresh produce, from our dairy cows to our apple orchids, all ingredients used in our recipes are home grown here on site. This familiarity with our ingredients has allowed us to perfect our recipes to be tasty and unique. So, if you would like to know exactly where your ingredients come from, how your food is made or if you are looking for a fun new hobby this is the place for you. All our recipes are 100% gluten free! If you are celiac, gluten intolerant or simply conscious of the food you put in your body you can sign up to our masterclass where you will learn to make some family favourites.

Fun Facts

Gluten is a protien found in wheat and other grains. A gluten free diet is the main treatment for celiac desease and gluten sensitivity. Some people believe a gluten free diet can help improve health proble.


Gluten can cause autoimmune disease, leaky gut and is a common cause of unexplained iron deficiency anemia.

Recipes

Mulit-Seed Loaf

Ingredients

  • Gluten free flour 450g
  • Salt 1 tbsp
  • Yeast 2 tbsp
  • Olive oil 6 tbsp
  • Free Range Eggs 2
  • Full Fat Milk 310ml
  • Caster Sugar 1tbsp
  • Cider Vinegar tsp
  • Mixed Seeds 4 tbsp

Steps

  1. Grease and line a 900g/2lb loaf tin.
  2. Place the flour into a free-standing food mixer fixed with the balloon whisk attachment.
  3. Add the salt, sugar and yeast in three separate piles.
  4. Mix together the milk, vinegar, oil, and two eggs in a jug.
  5. Pour the liquid into the mixing bowl and add the seeds.
  6. Switch on the mixer and whisk the ingredients for about 3-4 minutes.
  7. Put the batter into the prepared tin, cover with oiled cling film and leave to prove for about one hour, or until doubled in size.
  8. Preheat the oven to 180C. Bake for about 35-45 minutes. Cover with foil after ten minutes.
  9. Remove from the oven, leave to cool on a wire rack.

Brownies

Ingredients

  • Butter 200g
  • Dark Chocolate
  • Caster Sugar 225g
  • Eggs 3
  • Ground Almonds 100g

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.
  2. Butter a 4cm-deep, 20 x 20cm square cake tin and line with baking parchment.
  3. Melt 200g of the chocolate together with the butter in a large heatproof bowl set over a pan of just simmering water stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove from the heat and leave to cool for 10 mins.
  5. Whisk the sugar, eggs and a pinch of salt together in a large bowl until pale and foamy.
  6. Pour in the butter and chocolate mixture along with the almonds, then fold everything together until there are no streaks of chocolate.
  7. Tip the mixture into the tin, scatter over the rest of the chocolate.
  8. Bake for 25 mins, until cooked with a slight wobble in the middle.
  9. Leave to cool for 10 mins, then serve.

Pizza

Ingredients

Pizza Base
  • Golden Caster Sugar 2 tsp
  • Gluten Free Flour 400g
  • Salt 1 tsp
  • Baking Powder 2 tsp
  • Olive oil 5 tsp
  • Xantham Gum 1 tsp
Sauce & Toppings
  • Olive Oil 2 tbsp
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Chopped Tomatos 400g
  • Tomato Purée 2 tbsp
  • Caster Sugar 1tbsp
  • Basil Leaves, 5
  • Buffalo Mozzarella 250g

Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 220C and put two baking sheets inside.
  2. Make the sauce: heat the oil in a small saucepan and cook the onion with a generous pinch of salt for 10 mins over a low heat until softened.
    Add the chopped tomatoes, purée and sugar and bring to a gentle simmer.
    Cook, uncovered, for 25 - 30 mins or until reduced and thick, stirring regularly. Blitz the sauce with a hand blender until smooth. Season to taste and stir through the basil. Allow to cool a little.
  3. Make the dough: mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt and xanthan gum in a large mixing bowl. Make a well in the centre and pour in 250ml warm water and the olive oil. Combine quickly with your hands, to create a thick, wet, paste-like texture, adding an extra 20ml warm water if the dough feels a little dry. Store in an airtight container or covered bowl in the fridge for up to 24 hours before using. Lightly flour two more baking sheets. Split the dough into two and flatten with your fingers into 20 - 25cm rounds on the sheets.
  4. Finish the bases with a thin layer of the sauce and torn up mozzarella(You can add any other desired toppings). Place the baking sheets on top of the hot baking sheets in the oven and cook for 8 -10 mins or until crisp around the edges.
